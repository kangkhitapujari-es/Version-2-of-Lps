The #2 Cost Line Nobody Optimizes

Why SaaS Comp Playbooks from 2020 Are Breaking 2026 Margins

A panel for SaaS CFOs, CROs, and VP RevOps leaders who need to explain comp's margin impact to a board — and know where to start fixing it.

In the last 12 months, the CFO has moved from approving comp plans to questioning them. Efficient growth mandates, CAC payback scrutiny, and hiring freezes have put every dollar of sales compensation under board-level scrutiny it hasn't faced before.

The problem is that most plans were designed in 2020, when top-line ARR covered a lot of structural sloppiness. They weren't built to protect margin. They were built to accelerate revenue. Those are no longer the same instruction.

What's in it for you

Most comp plans aren't broken
in an obvious way.

RevOps teams do essential work, but too much of it is operating around a plan that was never designed for where the business is now. You're running 2020 playbooks: flat % of ARR regardless of deal quality, unlimited accelerators with no shape modeling, territory overlays that double-pay for the same dollar of revenue.

Matthew, Navin, and Jose aren't here to give you another framework. They're here to be specific about where 2020-era comp logic breaks 2026 margin math, where the real gains are, and what teams that have actually fixed it did differently.

You'll see how to move from a plan built around top-line ARR to one explicitly wired to your efficient growth model: CAC payback, gross margin, retention, and expansion. Without blowing up trust on the sales floor.

The 2020 assumptions that no longer hold, translated into the exact cost each one carries on your 2026 P&L
The Comp Maturity Curve, a 4-stage framework to self-place your plan, with the typical CFO reaction and rep floor sentiment at each stage
The Comp Audit, a working diagnostic to pressure-test whether your plan is paying for the behaviors your business actually needs
What margin-safe comp looks like in 2026, with the characteristics of top-tier SaaS comp drawn from Gong and 1Password's operating experience
First moves that don't require a full redesign, including quality gates, accelerator re-shaping, and a realistic timeline to start
Live Q&A, with CFO and CRO questions prioritized

You'll walk away with

Diagnose whether your current comp plan is paying for the behaviors that protect 2026 margins or the ones that erode them — using a 5-question audit you can run before the next board cycle

Identify the first design changes you can make before next year's plan is locked, without triggering a full redesign or losing rep trust mid-year
